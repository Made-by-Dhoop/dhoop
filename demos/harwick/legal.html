Legal & Privacy Policy

Who We Are

Regulatory Information

Harwick & Co Solicitors is authorised and regulated by the Solicitors Regulation Authority. You can check our details on the SRA register at sra.org.uk.

We are covered by professional indemnity insurance. Details of the insurer and coverage can be provided on request.

Privacy Policy

We take your privacy seriously and are committed to protecting your personal data. This policy explains how we collect, use, and protect information you provide to us.

What Information We Collect

When you contact us via our website, we collect:

  • Your name
  • Your email address
  • Your phone number (if provided)
  • Details of your legal enquiry

We do not collect payment information through this website.

How We Use Your Information

We use the information you provide solely to respond to your enquiry and, where you become a client, to carry out the legal services you've instructed us on. We do not use your information for marketing purposes or share it with third parties, except where required by law or regulation.

How Long We Keep Your Information

We retain client files for a minimum of six years after the conclusion of a matter, in line with our regulatory obligations. Contact enquiries that do not result in instructions are retained for 12 months and then securely deleted.

Your Rights

Under UK GDPR you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Request deletion of your data
  • Object to processing of your data
  • Request restriction of processing
  • Data portability

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at enquiries@harwicksolicitors.co.uk.

Cookies

This website does not use cookies or tracking technology.

Complaints

If you are unhappy with our service, please contact us in the first instance. If we cannot resolve your complaint, you can refer the matter to the Legal Ombudsman at legalombudsman.org.uk or the SRA at sra.org.uk.

If you are unhappy with how we have handled your personal data, you have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner's Office at ico.org.uk.
